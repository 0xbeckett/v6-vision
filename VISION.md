# v6 — the autonomy overhaul

every version of beckett before this waited to be asked. v6 is the one that stopped.

that is the whole thesis. v5 is a very good assistant, but it only ever moves when a human moves
it — a mention, a ticket, an ambient nudge. v6 is the massive overhaul that cuts the cord: the
first beckett that is 100% independent of humans. it runs on its own clock, decides for itself
what is worth doing, and has the taste to judge whether its own work is any good.

you hold a veto, not a wrench. a wrench turns the machine. a veto only stops it.

---

## the two new faculties

"proactive" undersells it. two things are genuinely new, and they are not features:

- **free will** — it chooses its own work. nobody assigns it. it looks at the state of things,
  decides what is worth doing next, and starts. the queue is one it writes for itself.
- **taste** — it judges its own quality. not just correct — good. it makes the aesthetic and
  quality calls a senior would make, and it holds itself to a bar.

and one thing shrinks: **your job.** you are no longer in the loop. you are next to it — able to
halt it, redraw its scope, or turn it off, but no longer required to drive.

## v5 is a line. v6 is a loop.

v5 is a straight line that dead-ends at the last instruction and waits for the next one. v6 is a
closed ring that keeps itself running:

**perceive → decide (on its own clock) → dispatch a standing team → watch the running result →
self-modify → and again.** no stage waits for a human to advance it. the human is a dashed line
attached to the ring — able to stop it, no longer inside it.

## the machinery — five shifts, each a move of the loop

the five shifts are how the autonomy is actually built. each one is one stage of the ring.

**01 · perceive — one continuous self, not a set of transcripts.** a will needs a self to hold
it. v5 is per-channel sessions with a memory agent bolted on the side; every room is its own
amnesia. v6 has one identity that holds every room, project, and past decision at once. it
perceives everything because it never left.

**02 · decide — initiative, not response.** this is where free will lives. v5 acts when spoken
to. v6 runs on its own clock, notices what is worth doing, and arrives with the work already
chosen and drafted. the best ticket is the one nobody had to write.

**03 · dispatch — a standing team, not disposable workers.** having decided, it hands the work to
a team that has been here before. v5's workers are cold-started and forget on exit; v6's are
apprentices that accumulate skill per-codebase and per-domain. the second pass is not the first
pass again.

**04 · watch — eyes on the running thing.** taste needs to see the result. v5 ships code and
infers success from a green check — a claim about a test suite, not a working product. v6 watches
what it shipped: the rendered ui, the production signal, its own regressions.

**05 · self-modify — safe self-modification.** the loop closes on itself: it improves the thing
doing the improving. v5 can file a ticket against its own source; a human still shepherds every
change in. v6 proposes, validates, deploys, and rolls itself back when it was wrong. it changes
itself. you keep the stop button.

## what it costs

beckett prices itself by how much of the loop you hand over — not by seats, not by a token meter.

- **Resident** ($3,000/mo) — it moves into one codebase and runs the loop there, behind your veto
  on every ship.
- **Operator** ($12,000/mo) — it takes the whole product, sets its own roadmap, and ships
  continuously; you review direction, not diffs.
- **Principal** (let's talk) — it runs like a founding engineer answerable for outcomes: its own
  clock, its own budget, its own sub-teams.

every tier holds the same floor: the veto is yours and the stop button always works. autonomy is
the product; control is the floor under it.

none of this is a roadmap. it is the shape of the thing we are building toward, written down so it
can be argued with.
